12:00 PM → 3:30 PM — ⭐ GULONG GORGE
🏞️ 古龙峡 · Glass Bridge · Kart Racing · Waterfall Walk
One of Guangdong's most dramatic day trips — a deep mountain gorge with a 1,314m glass suspension bridge (Yuntian Bridge), go-kart track, and a waterfall trail. Buy tickets at the gate — prices vary by package: glass bridge + canyon combo typically CNY 98–128/pax. Check current prices at the ticket counter on arrival. Suggested split: glass bridge first (everyone), then kart racing (whoever wants), then waterfall walk (younger ones — steep sections). Parents can rest at the food court area near the entrance while the younger group does the waterfall section — plenty of seating, drinks, and snacks available. Roughly 3–3.5 hrs inside. Leave by 3:30pm sharp to make it back for the evening.
Glass Bridge ~CNY 108/pax · Kart extra ⚠️ Leave by 3:30 PM — evening starts 6:30pm Parents rest at food court · No need to do waterfall section Opening hours 9am–4pm · Buy at gate

Walk out of Zumiao Station — breakfast shops are within 2–3 min on foot. Small shops around Zumiao station serve Foshan's famous breakfast specialties. 11:00 AM — Ancestral Temple
佛山祖庙 Foshan Ancestral Temple · Birthplace of Wing Chun
5-min walk from Zumiao Station — the breakfast shops and the temple are right next to each other. One of the most important Taoist temples in southern China, dating to 960 AD — and the birthplace of Wing Chun kung fu, made famous by Ip Man (Bruce Lee's teacher). There's a dedicated Wing Chun hall and Ip Man memorial inside. The live Wing Chun and Lion Dance performance is at 2:15pm daily in the courtyard — plan the whole morning around being back for this. It's 20 min and genuinely impressive.
CNY 20 entry · No passport needed ⭐ Wing Chun + Lion Dance: 2:15pm daily · arrive 10 min early

漓江 4-Star Cruise · 83km · The Scenery on China's 20 RMB Note
Already at Zhujiang Pier from the taxi — board the boat here. The highlight of the entire trip. You sail 83km from Guilin to Yangshuo through the most famous karst landscape on earth — the exact scenery printed on the Chinese 20 RMB banknote. Essential Tips
📱
DiDi + WeChat Pay

Set up both before landing. Link a foreign Visa/Mastercard to WeChat Pay at Changi Airport. DiDi accepts WeChat Pay. Most stalls, restaurants, and taxis are fully cashless in China now.

🌐
VPN + China SIM

Google, WhatsApp, Instagram, and Gmail are blocked in China. Buy a China SIM at Changi (Singtel/StarHub). Install a VPN (ExpressVPN) before landing — you cannot download it once inside China.

🗺️
Maps

Use Baidu Maps or Apple Maps offline. Google Maps works only with VPN. For DiDi, the app is in English and auto-translates addresses for the driver — just type destination in English.

🛂
No Visa Needed

Singapore passport = 30-day visa-free entry. Bring original passport — required for hotel check-in, train tickets, Canton Tower entry, and Pearl River cruise boarding. No exceptions.

💰
CNY Cash

Change SGD → CNY at Changi before flying (S$1 ≈ CNY 5.4). Bring ~CNY 500 cash/pax as emergency backup. WeChat Pay covers everything else. Most China ATMs accept foreign cards too.

🌡️
July Weather

GZ/SZX: 30–35°C, very humid. Guilin/Yangshuo: 28–33°C, afternoon rain possible (makes karst peaks beautifully misty). Pack: light clothes, foldable umbrella, SPF50+, portable USB fan, good walking shoes.

🚄
Train Booking

Book on Trip.com — English, accepts foreign cards. Open exactly 30 days before travel (July trains sell out in hours). Select 2nd class (二等座). Same passport number at booking and at station entry.

🍺
Yangshuo Warning

West Street restaurants = tourist trap prices, mediocre food. Beer fish on Hospital Road (医院路) = real price, real taste. Always ask "多少钱一斤?" (price per 500g?) before ordering live fish.
